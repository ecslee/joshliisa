Our Story

Once upon a time, Josh and Aliisa didn't know each other. Weird! Fortunately, in the fall of 2014, our paths crossed through shared social groups and the same undergraduate Christian ministry. As we got to know each other, through everything from rock-climbing to bothering our friends together, things just clicked!

In the fall of 2015, Josh headed out to graduate school at UC Santa Barbara and left Aliisa to finish her senior year in chilly New Jersey. Still, even while we were on opposite coasts, our relationship flourished and deepened through many, many, many, many, many, many, many, many, many, many, many, many, many Skype calls and all the cross-country visits we could sneak in. At the end of May 2016, Josh flew out to Princeton to celebrate Aliisa's graduation... and ask her something very special.

Now we're in our last stretch of long-distance as Aliisa finishes a year-long fellowship at International Schools Services, a non-profit in the Princeton area. We're pretty excited to walk down the aisle this summer, then move to Santa Barbara to start a new life together!

Our Wedding Venue

Moorestown Community House

The Community House is a beautiful brick and stone building, built in 1926 right on Moorestown's Main Street. Today, it is a non-profit that serves the area for a wide range of community events. We'll have the building to ourselves for the afternoon; we'll plan to do the ceremony on the beautiful front lawn, then move inside to the ballroom for our reception. Take a look at their site and location!

Things To Do

in the Princeton area

Staying in Princeton before the wedding? Check out some of Josh and Aliisa's favorite things to do in the area! (Click each option to see more info)

Princeton University

Princeton University, where we met, is a beautiful place. We recommend you start by Nassau hall, the oldest building on campus, and pay attention for ivy-lined walls and our famous black squirrels.

Princeton Art Museum

While walking around campus, be sure to stop at the Princeton University Art Museum, right in the heart of campus. The museum contains an amazing collection of classical and contemporary art and admission is completely free.

Here we are the month we first started dating!

Nassau Street & Palmer Square

Just north of campus, you'll find a host of walkable local shops and restaurants. Some of our favorite places to eat are Teresa's Caffe (Italian), Hoagie Haven (Princeton’s college town classic), Mamoun's Falafel (Mediterranean), Witherspoon Grill (American), and Sakura Express (Sushi). Afterwards, head to Halo Pub for ice cream or Small World for coffee. The Princeton University Store is also right on Nassau for souvenirs and gifts.

Tow Path & Carnegie Lake

Most of the photos you see on this site were taken on the peaceful and scenic tow path. This tree-shaded path runs along Lake Carnegie and is perfect for a summer walk or run. If you are driving, there is a parking lot with access on Harrison street, just South of Lake Carnegie.

Terhune Orchards

Terhune Orchards is a 200-acre farm with lovely green walks, fresh produce, and famous apple cider donuts.

Grounds for Sculpture

The Grounds for Sculpture is a 42-acre sculpture garden located in Hamilton, a 15 minute drive from Princeton. This is a strange and wonderful place, filled with hundreds of beautiful, thought-provoking, and often confusing sculptures. This has been one of our favorite ways to spend an afternoon – make sure to bring a camera! Adult admission is $18.
